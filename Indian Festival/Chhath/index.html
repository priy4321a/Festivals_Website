About Chhath Puja

Chhath Puja is an ancient Hindu festival dedicated to the Sun God, Surya, and Chhathi Maiya. It is mainly celebrated in Bihar, Jharkhand, Uttar Pradesh, and Nepal. Devotees offer prayers and fasting to seek blessings for their well-being and prosperity. The festival includes bathing in holy rivers, fasting, and offering "Arghya" (water and milk offerings) to the Sun at sunrise and sunset.

How People Celebrate

Sun Worship

Devotees gather by rivers or ponds to offer water and milk to the rising and setting Sun to seek blessings.

Fasting

Participants observe rigorous fasting during the festival, consuming only fruits and water as they pray for the well-being of their loved ones.

Community Gathering

People come together to celebrate Chhath Puja by preparing traditional foods and joining community prayers.
